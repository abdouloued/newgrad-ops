# Amazon

## Format

- **Stages:** Recruiter screen (15–30 min, light) → Online Assessment → Technical phone screen (often skipped for strong new-grad OA scores) → Virtual onsite loop (4–5 rounds)
- **Coding tools:** HackerRank for the OA; CoderPad-equivalent shared editor for live rounds
- **Coding style:** Classic LeetCode-style algorithmic problems for the coding portions — but Amazon weights the **Leadership Principles** behavioral evaluation as heavily as the coding bar, more than any other company in this repo's coverage. The OA itself has three parts: 2 LeetCode-style coding problems (medium–hard), a scenario-based "work simulation" judgment section, and a workstyle survey tied to the Leadership Principles.
- **Lookups allowed:** No live-interview lookups; OA is closed-book
- **AI usage:** Not officially published; treat as no-AI unless told otherwise

## Focus areas

- Standard DSA: arrays/hashing, trees, graphs, DP — medium–hard difficulty
- Object-oriented design or light system design depending on level (more common at senior new-grad / L5 bar)
- **Leadership Principles fit** — this isn't a soft add-on, it's scored as its own bar. One round (the "Bar Raiser") is almost entirely behavioral and the Bar Raiser interviewer can override the rest of the loop's decision.
- A dedicated or blended "GenAI fluency" round has started appearing in onsite loops — be ready to discuss how you've used AI tools in your own work, critically (tradeoffs, not just "I used Copilot").

## Behavioral

Built entirely around the **16 Leadership Principles** (Customer Obsession, Ownership, Bias for Action, Dive Deep, Are Right A Lot, etc. — the current list is on Amazon's careers site). Expect every interviewer, including technical ones, to ask at least one Leadership-Principle-framed question.

Suggested STAR stories — prepare at least one per principle group:
1. A time you owned a problem beyond your assigned scope (Ownership)
2. A time you made a decision with incomplete data and it worked out, or didn't (Bias for Action / Are Right A Lot)
3. A time you went deeper into a problem than your role technically required (Dive Deep)
4. A time you advocated for a user/customer over an easier internal solution (Customer Obsession)

## Recommended prep

**Coding:**
- Standard NeetCode 150 / Blind 75 coverage — Amazon's coding bar is closer to Google/Meta than to Anthropic/OpenAI/Stripe
- Practice explaining Big-O and tradeoffs out loud; interviewers probe reasoning, not just the final answer

**Behavioral — don't under-prep this:**
- Write out a story for each Leadership Principle, not just 3–4 generic ones. Reusing one story across principles is fine if it genuinely fits, but you need coverage.
- The Bar Raiser round is not a formality — treat it with the same prep weight as a coding round.
