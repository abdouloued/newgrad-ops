# Netflix

## Format

- **Stages:** Recruiter screen → Technical screen (CoderPad or CodeSignal) → Hiring manager interview → Virtual onsite (coding, system design, data modeling, behavioral rounds) — some candidates report under 3 weeks recruiter-screen to final onsite
- **Coding tools:** CodeSignal or CoderPad, monitored platforms, not a whiteboard
- **Coding style:** Medium–hard problems focused on concurrency, caching, and data structures, but **weighted lightest of the three round types.** After your initial solution, expect open-ended follow-ups about tradeoffs and failure modes rather than a second coding problem — Netflix cares more about how you reason about a design than raw solve speed.
- **Lookups allowed:** Live rounds are closed-book/monitored
- **AI usage:** Not officially published; for internships, some candidates report an "AI-assisted coding evaluation" as part of the OA itself (i.e. a round that explicitly involves AI tools) — this is unusual relative to every other company in this repo, so don't assume it applies to your specific loop. Confirm with your recruiter rather than assuming either way.

## Focus areas

- **Behavioral carries the most weight of any company in this repo's coverage** — explicitly more than coding. System design is next, coding is weighted least.
- Concurrency, caching, and core data structures for the coding portion
- Tradeoff articulation — explaining *why* a design choice was made, not just producing working code
- Data modeling for roles touching Netflix's content/personalization systems

## Behavioral

Built around Netflix's public **"People Over Process"** culture memo (formerly "Freedom & Responsibility") — this is an unusually detailed, official public document and worth reading directly, not just summarized:
- **Judgment** — Netflix's stated policy for things like travel/expenses is literally "use your best judgment"; interviewers probe whether you can be trusted with autonomy
- **Candor** — direct, specific feedback is core to the culture; behavioral answers that show you can give/receive blunt feedback land well
- **Impact over effort** — Netflix's memo explicitly values outcomes over hours worked or process followed

Suggested STAR stories:
1. A time you were given unusual autonomy and how you used it responsibly
2. A time you gave or received direct, possibly uncomfortable feedback
3. A time you made a judgment call without a formal process to fall back on

## Recommended prep

**Coding:**
- Standard NeetCode 150 coverage is enough — don't over-index on coding prep relative to behavioral prep here, that's the opposite of where Netflix actually weights its loop
- Practice narrating tradeoffs out loud after you have a working solution — that's explicitly part of the evaluation, not a bonus

**Behavioral — this is the one to actually spend the most time on:**
- Read the full "People Over Process" memo at jobs.netflix.com/culture before your interview — it's short, public, and the closest thing to an answer key for what they're evaluating
- Prepare stories that show independent judgment, not just "followed the process correctly"
