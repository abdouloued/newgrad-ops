# Microsoft

## Format

- **Stages:** Recruiter phone screen → Online Assessment → Hiring manager interview (light technical, mostly behavioral) → Interview loop (4–5 rounds: coding, design, behavioral)
- **Coding tools:** Codility most commonly for the OA (also HackerRank for some teams); auto-graded and screen-recorded
- **Coding style:** Classic LeetCode-style algorithmic problems, similar bar to Google/Meta/Amazon — but each coding round typically opens with a behavioral warm-up and an open-ended knowledge question before the algorithm problem, so the round isn't pure DSA from minute one.
- **Lookups allowed:** OA is closed-book and screen-recorded; live rounds generally closed-book
- **AI usage:** Not officially published; treat as no-AI unless told otherwise
- **OA passing bar:** Reportedly need roughly 60%+ to advance — usually means fully passing most problems' test cases, not just attempting all of them

## Focus areas

- Strings & arrays: sliding window, two pointers, prefix sum
- Trees & graphs: DFS/BFS, BST properties
- Hash maps & linked lists: reversal, cycle detection, frequency counting
- A debugging section is common on the OA — you're given buggy code and asked to fix it, which rewards reading-comprehension of unfamiliar code as much as writing from scratch
- Basic SQL shows up on some OAs

## Behavioral

Less codified than Amazon's Leadership Principles, but consistently emphasized for new grads:
- **Soft skills and teamwork** — at entry level, Microsoft explicitly weighs "good teammate, curious, can reason abstractly" alongside raw coding ability
- **Growth mindset** — comfort saying "I don't know yet, here's how I'd find out"
- **Clear communication** — the open-ended knowledge question at the start of coding rounds is partly testing how you explain your thinking, not just what you know

Suggested STAR stories:
1. A time you had to learn an unfamiliar codebase or technology quickly
2. A time you gave or received critical feedback well
3. A time abstract problem-solving mattered more than a specific language/tool you knew

## Recommended prep

**Coding:**
- Standard NeetCode 150 / Blind 75 coverage, same tier as Google/Meta/Amazon
- Practice reading and fixing unfamiliar/buggy code, not just writing from a blank file — the debugging-section format rewards this specifically
- Brush up on basic SQL (joins, aggregates) even for non-data roles — it shows up unexpectedly on some OAs

**Behavioral:**
- Prepare to narrate your thought process out loud during the "open-ended knowledge question" warm-up, not just during the algorithm portion
