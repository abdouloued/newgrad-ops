# Meta

## Format

- **Stages:** Application → OA (HackerRank) → Phone screen → Virtual onsite (3–4 rounds)
- **Coding tools:** CoderPad for live interviews; HackerRank for the OA
- **Coding style:** Classic LeetCode-style algorithmic problems, same family as Google's — the difference is speed pressure, not problem style. Expect to be scored partly on how fast you reach a working solution, not just correctness.
- **Lookups allowed:** Generally no; OA may allow docs
- **AI usage:** Not permitted unless explicitly stated

## Focus areas

- Speed and accuracy on medium/hard DSA — Meta is known for high bar on coding speed
- Graphs (especially BFS/DFS), trees, arrays, two pointers, DP
- Product sense for APM and product-adjacent roles
- Systems design for E4+ (not typically required for new grad)

## Behavioral

Uses the **"Tell me about a time..." format** — STAR method expected:
- **Impact** — quantify results wherever possible
- **Collaboration** — building consensus, resolving conflict
- **Learning** — growing from failure or feedback
- **Speed** — bias toward action and iteration

Suggested STAR stories:
1. A time you delivered something under a tight deadline
2. A time you turned a negative situation around
3. A time you worked across teams to ship something

## Recommended prep

**Coding:**
- Practice speed — aim for mediums under 20 minutes
- Focus on: BFS/DFS, binary search, linked lists, trees, sliding window
- NeetCode 150 full coverage + some hards for graphs and DP

**Behavioral:**
- Prepare 5–6 stories adaptable to different questions
- Quantify impact in each story (users affected, time saved, error rate reduced)
