# STAR Story Template

Use this to prepare 5–8 versatile behavioral stories before interviews.
Each story should be adaptable to multiple question types.

---

## Story: [Descriptive title, e.g. "API reliability project at [Company]"]

**Situation:**
[2–3 sentences. What was the context? What was the state of things before you acted?
Set the scene without over-explaining.]

**Task:**
[1–2 sentences. What was your specific responsibility? What needed to happen?]

**Action:**
[3–5 sentences. What did YOU do? Focus on your decisions and actions, not the team's.
Use "I" not "we" where your individual contribution matters.
Include: what you decided, why you decided it, what you did step by step.]

**Result:**
[2–3 sentences. What was the outcome? Quantify wherever possible.
Include: metric improved, time saved, users affected, problem solved, lesson learned.]

## Common behavioral question types

**Leadership / Initiative:**
- "Tell me about a time you took initiative without being asked."
- "Describe a time you led a project."

**Failure / Learning:**
- "Tell me about a time you failed."
- "Describe a mistake you made and what you learned."

**Conflict:**
- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate or manager."
- "How do you handle pushback on your ideas?"

**Ambiguity:**
- "Tell me about a time you worked on something without clear requirements."
- "How do you handle uncertainty?"

**Impact:**
- "What's the most impactful thing you've worked on?"
- "Tell me about a project you're most proud of."

**Collaboration:**
- "Tell me about a time you worked closely with someone very different from you."
