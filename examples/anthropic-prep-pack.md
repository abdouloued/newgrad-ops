## APPLICATION

**Role types to target:** Software Engineer, New Grad · Research Engineer · Infrastructure Engineer

**Resume focus:**
- Python projects with reliability signals (tests, metrics, error handling)
- Anything touching AI tooling, evals, developer tools, or observability
- Avoid vague bullet points — replace with decisions and outcomes

**Portfolio signal:**
- Projects that show you can build and maintain something non-trivial
- Tests and documentation are strong signals at Anthropic
- An evals or benchmarking component stands out for research-adjacent roles

**Fit score tip:** Add a sentence to your resume summary about why you care about safe, beneficial AI — but make it specific and genuine, not generic.

## OA PREP

**Focus patterns** (based on tooling/infra role signals):
1. Sliding Window — appears frequently; tests string/array manipulation under constraints
2. Graphs — DFS/BFS, connected components, topological sort
3. Dynamic Programming — coin change, LIS, word break

## BEHAVIORAL

**Core themes Anthropic looks for:**
- **Ownership** — shipped something without being asked, or took responsibility when things went wrong
- **Judgment under ambiguity** — made a good call with incomplete information
- **Intellectual curiosity** — went deeper than the task required
- **Mission alignment** — real, specific reasons for caring about AI safety

**STAR stories to prepare:**
1. A time you shipped something without clear requirements (ownership + ambiguity)
2. A time you disagreed with a decision and how you handled it (judgment + collaboration)
3. A time you debugged something you didn't understand at first (curiosity + persistence)

**"Why Anthropic" prep:**
- Read at least one Anthropic research post (Constitutional AI, model cards, alignment work)
- Have a specific, genuine answer to "why now" and "why Anthropic over other AI labs"
- Connect your technical background to Anthropic's specific product or research area
